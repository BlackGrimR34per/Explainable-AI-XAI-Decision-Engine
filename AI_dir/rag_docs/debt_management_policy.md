A Debt Service Ratio (DSR) below 40% is considered healthy and manageable.
DSR between 40-60% indicates moderate debt burden requiring careful assessment.
DSR above 60% is considered high risk and may lead to rejection.

Post-loan DSR must not exceed 60% to ensure sustainable repayment.
Applicants with post-loan DSR above 70% are typically rejected unless strong compensating factors exist.

Monthly loan instalment should not exceed 30% of gross monthly income.
Instalment-to-income ratios above 40% indicate potential repayment difficulty.
